Why Graphviz Still Matters

A deep dive into the classic tool that started it all.

The OG of Visualization

In a world of WebGL, D3.js, and Three.js, it is easy to overlook Graphviz. Born at AT&T Labs, Graphviz introduced the DOT language—a simple, text-based way to describe graphs. "A -> B". That simplicity is its superpower.

Structure First, Style Second

Many modern tools get bogged down in styling—colors, shadows, animations. Graphviz forces you to think about structure first. What is the hierarchy? What are the dependencies? The algorithm (dot, neato, fdp) takes care of the layout. This separation of concerns is a core principle of good ontology design.

Integration Everywhere

Because it is text-based and command-line driven, Graphviz is embedded in thousands of pipelines. It visualizes Terraform plans, huge neural network architectures, and package dependencies. It is the plumbing of the visual web. For any ontologist, understanding DOT is as essential as understanding SQL.
